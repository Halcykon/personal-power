# ✅ Action &amp; Habit Tracker

Every exercise, in one place. Check items off as you complete them. (On GitHub these checkboxes are clickable inside Issues/PRs; in the repo view and on the site they're a manual reference — tick them in your own copy or your journal.)

## Foundational habits — carry these through all 30 days

- [ ] Keep a daily **Success Journal**: capture what you learn and your reflections.
- [ ] Do one part per day, then do its exercise the same day.
- [ ] Whenever you decide something, take one small action on it *immediately* to lock in momentum.

## Part 1 — Decide and act

- [ ] Write down two actions or decisions you've been putting off that would raise your quality of life.
- [ ] Decide on both — cut off the other options.
- [ ] Take action on at least one of them *right now*: make the call, send the message, start the task.

## Part 2 — Re-wire pain &amp; pleasure

Write **four NEW** postponed actions in your journal (different from Day 1). For each:

- [ ] Write (1) the action, (2) the pain you've linked to doing it, (3) the pleasure you get from *not* doing it.
- [ ] Write (4) what it will cost you over the next 2–5 years if you don't change — emotionally, self-image, energy, self-esteem, finances, relationships. Make it emotional.
- [ ] Write (5) everything you'll gain by acting now — a big, emotional list.
- [ ] Take action on at least one immediately.

## Part 3 — Spot your neuro-associations

- [ ] Write 6 destiny-shaping associations: 3 empowering ones from your past, 3 still limiting you today. Just identify them.

## Part 4 — Change the association (NAC)

- [ ] For each thing you want to change, write 10 reasons you *must* change now (cost of not changing, gains of changing) + why you can.
- [ ] Design 4–5 pattern interrupts to break the old pattern.
- [ ] Repeatedly imagine and *feel* the pleasure of the new behavior until it sticks.

## Part 5 — Manage your state

- [ ] Describe something inspiring twice — once flat, once with full passion — and journal the differences in body, breath, face, and voice.
- [ ] During the day, rate your state 0–10 and snap into a stronger state by changing your body.

## Part 8 — Empowering questions

- [ ] Write 5 morning questions; ask them every morning for the rest of the program, with 2+ *felt* answers each.
- [ ] Catch and replace one disempowering question you ask habitually.

## Part 9 — Values &amp; beliefs

- [ ] List and rank your toward-values (ends, not means) via "What's most important to me in life?"
- [ ] List and rank your away-from values.
- [ ] Surface the rules under each, name limiting global beliefs, and rewrite the rules so success and love are easier.

## Part 10 — Change a belief (Dickens pattern)

- [ ] Write 2 limiting beliefs; feel their pain at 5 / 10 / 20 years, then break state.
- [ ] Write 2 empowering replacement beliefs; run their pleasure out 5 / 10 / 20 years.
- [ ] Journal how each new belief enhances your life.

## Part 11 — Build the pressure for goals

- [ ] Journal the areas of life you're dissatisfied with (across all categories).
- [ ] Write 2–3 new beliefs you'd need to actually set and achieve your goals.

## Part 12 — The goal-setting workshop

- [ ] Brainstorm goals without limits in 3 categories (personal development, things, economic).
- [ ] Timeline each (1/3/5/10/20 yrs); pick your top three one-year goals per category; write *why* for each.
- [ ] Take one immediate action on a goal; keep your top goals visible daily.

## Part 15 — Emotional rituals

- [ ] List 5 negative emotions and write each one's step-by-step recipe.
- [ ] List 5 positive emotions and their recipes; design a pattern interrupt for each negative one.
- [ ] Chunk down how you procrastinate and build a way to break it.

## Part 16 — Anchoring

- [ ] Create one strong positive anchor: choose the emotion, relive peak moments, link a unique trigger *at the peak*, repeat.
- [ ] Do at least 15 swish patterns from procrastination to your committed self.

## Part 17 — Financial checkup

- [ ] Answer one question per "wealth wound": limiting beliefs; an abundance number made a *must*; a strategy; follow-through; what to learn; a vigilance statement; a supporting identity.
- [ ] Take one financial action today and one act of giving this week.

## Part 18 — End financial self-sabotage

- [ ] Journal: the pain of lacking abundance; free-associate to "money"; money phrases from childhood; the pleasures of abundance; rewrite/ridicule each limiting belief.
- [ ] 10-Day Financial Challenge: three new money-making ideas a day for 10 days.

## Part 19 — Conquer fear

- [ ] Write your current rules for success and failure; question whose they are; write 2 new empowering definitions.
- [ ] Run the erasure technique on a past failure (rewind fast, cartoon sounds); anchor and rehearse success; dissolve a rejection the same way.
- [ ] List 2 past failures + 2 rejections and extract a benefit from each.

## Part 22 — Bank your confidence

- [ ] Write a paragraph on each of your 5 greatest successes — especially ones that looked dark but you pulled off — as proof you can feel confident on demand.

## Part 23 — Eat live foods

- [ ] Audit everything you ate in the last 24 hours; circle the water-rich items; estimate your percentage.
- [ ] 10-day challenge: 70%+ water-rich foods (a salad each meal, fruit snacks) + the breathing ratio (inhale 1 / hold 4 / exhale 2, ×10, three times a day).

## Part 24 — Relationships

- [ ] If single, write what you *do* and *don't* want in a partner; if partnered, define what the relationship is and isn't about.
- [ ] Make a list of unique, spontaneous things to do, say, and create together.

## Part 25 — Solve problems fast

- [ ] Write Tony's 5 problem-solving questions, then pick a current problem and run it through all five in writing.

## Part 26 — Mastery

- [ ] Redo any skipped exercises; re-listen to the six-human-needs / Power interview material.
- [ ] Pick your top four one-year goals + write *why*; build a plan with a first step within 4 days.
- [ ] Keep doing your morning questions for 10 days; start a permanent journal habit (CANI).
