# Day 17 · Unlocking the Financial Genius Within — Healing the Seven Wealth Wounds

> 📄 **[Read the full cleaned transcript →](../transcripts/day-17.md)**  ·  🏠 **[Home](../README.md)**

**Core message:** If you don't have enough money, there's "one and only one primary reason" — you haven't **conditioned yourself for wealth.** Money is just "a means to measure the exchange of value between people," but most of us carry **wealth wounds**: negative associations and missing strategies that make us sabotage abundance even when we know how to create it. Heal the seven wounds and you change your financial future — but never forget you are *bigger than your finances.*

## The big ideas

- **Money is emotionally charged** — people swing between excitement and contempt ("something evil or suspect"). That contempt usually shows up as a lack of economic wellbeing. Strip the baggage and money is simply a **representation of exchanged value**.
- **The #1 reason people fail financially: negative associations.** We claim we want money, but we hold an **approach–avoidance** wiring — linking pain to "excess" (you'll work too hard, have no time for family, lose your spirituality, be judged, pay more taxes, get audited). The brain gets scared as money arrives and sabotages it.
- **Lack doesn't ennoble.** "Wherever I see lack, I see crime en masse… drugs… people taking advantage." Survival keeps the focus on *you*; abundance frees you to ask "how can I contribute?" *(He's careful: money isn't the only answer, and many contribute from nothing.)*
- **The Seven Wealth Wounds:**
  1. **Negative associations to having money** (the big one).
  2. **Never making abundance an absolute MUST** — survival is a must, abundance isn't, so it never happens.
  3. **No effective strategy** — passion alone fails: "if you're running east looking for a sunset, you've got a problem."
  4. **Failure to follow the plan consistently** — the daily follow-through, not the knowing.
  5. **Relying on experts** instead of building your own expertise (especially on taxes).
  6. **Financial complacency** — resting on your laurels; "whatever you fail to use, you lose."
  7. **Letting financial crisis become financial ruin** — the "dagger that will murder your financial future."
- **Three strategies to master (wound #3):** (1) *attract* income, (2) *manage/invest* it so "money is your slave instead of you being the slave to money," (3) *share* it, because the brain must link pleasure to wealth or it won't keep working for it.
- **Modeling is the method** — find people who built wealth on their own and study exactly what they do daily. Become "a hunter… your prey is now wealth" — stalk it, then "don't kill it, make it your pet."
- **Be your own expert.** Roughly **one-third of lifetime earnings** goes to taxes — more than you'll spend on your kids' college. Don't overpay out of ignorance; the IRS comes after *you*, not your accountant.
- **Gratitude is the essence of real wealth.** Sir John Templeton's answer to "what does it take to be wealthy?" was one word: **gratitude.** "When you're grateful, you're rich" — and "money will make you more of who you are."

## Memorable lines & metaphors

- "Get your **money to be your slave** instead of you being the slave to money."
- The **hunter and the pet:** stalk wealth like prey, then "don't kill it — make it your pet… and this pet will take really good care of you."
- "If you're running **east looking for a sunset**, you've got a problem" — passion without strategy.
- **Financial bloodletting:** unhealed wounds "drip out" your wellbeing every single day.
- **The Parable of the Talents** (5 / 2 / 1): "those who do well with what they've got will be given more" — and the servant who buried his talent lost it. "You don't have enough *not* to invest."
- "Be like a **rubber ball and bounce back up**." Babe Ruth "struck out more times than anybody… but he also got more home runs."
- "The **past does not equal the future**" — applied to your finances.
- "More **month at the end of the money**."
- Templeton's test: "When you're grateful, you're rich" — money without gratitude leaves you "emotionally poor."

## The Day 17 exercise

Take out your **Success Journal** and give yourself a **financial checkup** against all seven wealth wounds — handle each problem *before* it comes up. Write your answers to these seven questions:

1. **Beliefs:** What beliefs do I have that are probably keeping me from increasing my wealth and attracting abundance? Identify them.
2. **Must:** Do I have a *specific* amount of money — well beyond bare necessities — that represents massive abundance and that I'm absolutely committed to as a **must**? If not, design that number now and make it important enough to happen no matter what.
3. **Strategy:** Do I have an effective strategy to build my wealth and surpass those goals? If no, **commit to do something today** — write, call, or talk to someone — to learn the initial steps. "Don't put it off even one day."
4. **Follow-through:** Have I gotten myself to follow through consistently in the past? If no, use what you're learning here to take a little action each day, each building on the last.
5. **Self-reliance:** What do I need to learn (taxes, etc.) to make my own financial decisions with good input? Make a list of what you don't understand and go find someone who can answer it.
6. **Vigilance:** Write a sentence or two making it clear *why* you'll keep following through even once you start to succeed — so you don't get complacent and "give up the power to somebody else."
7. **Faith/identity:** Develop and write a belief that supports you — for Tony: that **who you are is much bigger than anything that could ever happen to you financially**, bigger than the economy.

Then **take one action today** — a phone call, a book, a tape, an investment in yourself — *and* go **do something for someone else** in the next three or four days (give time, energy, or money) to teach your brain "there's more than enough."

## Make it stick — how to apply it, plus honest context

**What modern psychology backs up**

- Doing a **belief checkup** before a crisis is real **anticipatory coping / implementation intentions** — deciding in advance beats willpower in the moment.
- The pull of **negative money associations** is genuine: money beliefs and financial anxiety measurably shape financial behavior ("money scripts" in financial-psychology research).
- "Follow the plan consistently / small daily amounts" is straight **behavioral habit formation** — and the compound-interest point is just arithmetic, well supported.
- **Gratitude** is one of the better-evidenced wellbeing interventions, and **prosocial giving** reliably boosts mood and a sense of abundance.
- **Modeling** the financially successful echoes **Bandura's social learning** — copy *process and habits*, not just visible outcomes.

**Where to keep your skepticism**

- The compound-interest figures are **garbled and rely on a 16% "secure" return** — unrealistic today, and the read-aloud numbers (e.g. "$3.25/day = $100/month") don't reconcile, with one 25-year total clearly a transcription slip. Treat the *principle* of consistent investing as sound; ignore the specific rates and totals.
- "Anyone can earn **over 20% annually in a very secured way**" is a classic **sales overreach** — there's no safe 20%+ return; be wary of anyone promising it.
- "Your finances fail for **one and only one** reason — your conditioning" ignores **wages, debt, and structural circumstance.** Use it to find your *internal* blocks, not to blame yourself for every hardship.
- The biographical claims — **$1M net worth "in less than 12 months," the "castle," "$758,000 in debt," feeding "125,000 people in 65 cities," the Beardstown Ladies' "25% over 10 years"** — are **unverifiable motivational anecdotes.** (The Beardstown Ladies' returns were later *publicly debunked* as a miscalculation; the real figure was far lower.) Take them as inspiration, not evidence.
- The repeated **financial-mastery seminar pitch** is overt advertising — he admits "I guess I am" promoting it. The teaching stands without the upsell.

**Apply it now:** don't just read the seven questions — *write* the answers, especially #1 (your limiting beliefs) and #2 (a specific abundance number you'll treat as a must). Then do the smallest possible thing today (one call, one book) and one act of giving this week — the giving is what rewires "there's more than enough."
