# Day 12 · The Goal-Setting Workshop

> 📄 **[Read the full cleaned transcript →](../transcripts/day-12.md)**  ·  🏠 **[Home](../README.md)**

**Core message:** This is the day you design your future on paper. In a real-time, write-while-you-listen "workshop (or fun shop)," you brainstorm goals without limits, put a deadline on each — *"goals are nothing but dreams with a deadline"* — and most importantly attach a compelling **reason** to the ones that matter. Because **reasons come first, answers come second**: you don't need to know *how* yet, only to be committed enough that you'll find a way.

## The big ideas

- **Real-time by design.** Earlier tapes saved exercises for the end and research showed people didn't follow through. So today you do it *while it plays* — pen moving the whole time the music runs. Don't do it in the car; take the phone off the hook; don't get interrupted; don't get lazy.
- **You're not lazy — you just have infinite goals.** The fix is goals with *power* behind them.
- **Come from a child's frame.** A kid writing to Santa has no trouble asking for "two swimming pools"; an adult says "you'd be lucky to get a tub in the backyard." Start from absolute expectation without limits — **you can get practical later.**
- **Three categories of goals:**
  1. **Personal development** — emotional, mental, social, spiritual, physical; skills, character, places, experiences. *Who are you committed to becoming?* These determine lifelong happiness.
  2. **Thing goals** — a "shopping list of fun"; pure pleasure is allowed. Money is no different from any other abundance you let yourself have.
  3. **Economic / financial goals** — income, net worth, business, giving, investments, retirement.
- **The workshop pattern (run on each category):** brainstorm fast → put a timeline on every item (1, 3, 5, 10, or 20 years) → pick your **top three one-year goals** → write a paragraph on **why you're absolutely committed.** Result: **nine one-year goals**, each with a reason. Keep them where you'll see them daily (inside the cover of your success journal).
- **Make it real until your brain believes it.** When a goal feels as if you already have it, "something clicks" and it often manifests fast. *(He flags this himself as sounding metaphysical.)*
- **Never leave the site of a goal without taking action** toward it — to build momentum.
- **The carrot *and* the stick.** Know both the pleasure of achieving and the price of *not* achieving — the double push that makes you follow through.

## The Day 12 exercise

Set up first: be at home (not driving), phone off the hook, no interruptions, success journal open, **pen moving the entire time**. Then, for **each** of the three categories — personal development, thing goals, economic goals — run the same four steps:

1. **Brainstorm without limits** for the timed period — write as fast as you can, abbreviate, never stop. (Personal development: ~6 minutes; thing goals: ~2.5 minutes; economic goals: ~2 minutes.)
2. **Timeline everything:** beside each item write **1, 3, 5, 10, or 20** — the number of years (or less) within which you're committed to achieving it.
3. **Pick your top three one-year goals** in that category — the ones that genuinely excite and compel you — and rewrite them on a fresh page.
4. **Write the *why*:** under each of those three, write a paragraph on why you're **absolutely committed** to achieving it within the year — strong enough that you'll follow through.

You now have **nine one-year goals with reasons.** Then:

5. **Take immediate action** — beside each of the nine, write at least one activity you can do **right now or within 12–24 hours**, and actually do it today (make the call, book the lesson, start the diet tonight, request the brochure / sit in the car).
6. **Run the rocking-chair test** on your goals — feel both the cost of *not* achieving them and the joy of having achieved them.
7. **Keep the nine goals visible daily** (inside the journal cover or somewhere you'll see them every day).

## Make it stick — how to apply it, plus honest context

**What modern psychology backs up**

- **Deadlines and specificity work.** "Dreams with a deadline" tracks with **goal-setting theory** (Locke & Latham): specific, time-bound goals beat vague "do your best" intentions.
- **Reasons before methods** maps onto **intrinsic motivation** and connecting actions to a deeper "why" — autonomous motivation predicts follow-through far better than willpower alone.
- **Take action immediately** is sound behavior change: small **implementation intentions** ("when X, I'll do Y") and early momentum make a goal far more likely to survive contact with real life.
- **The rocking-chair test** is essentially **mental contrasting / "playing the tape forward"** — pairing the desired future with the cost of inaction, a validated motivational technique.
- **Vivid mental rehearsal** of *how* you'll pursue a goal has real attentional and motivational benefits.

**Where to keep your skepticism**

- **The lottery stories are the big one.** A woman who "won exactly $100,000" in six and a half weeks, then $200,000; a Detroit couple who told everyone they'd already won and then "won over $1 million" on the third try — these are **unverifiable, extraordinary anecdotes**, and Tony himself says he doesn't buy lottery tickets and isn't telling you to set lottery goals. Treat them as motivation, **not** evidence that belief produces jackpots.
- **"Make it so real your brain believes it already happened and it manifests"** drifts into **law-of-attraction territory.** Visualization can boost motivation and direct attention — but *visualizing outcomes alone* can actually reduce effort. Pair imagery with planning and action (which, to his credit, today's exercise does).
- **Timelines you "don't know how" to hit** can inspire — or set you up for discouragement. Use the commitment to mobilize, but let step 3 from Day 1 (notice your results) keep your plan honest.

**Apply it now:** block 30–45 uninterrupted minutes, do the full brainstorm → timeline → top-three → why loop for all three categories, and before you stand up, take *one* concrete action on *one* goal. Momentum today is the whole point.

**Looking ahead:** the next two days are conditioning with your **subliminals**, with no new tape until **Day 15 (Monday)**. Tony also previews a weekend **bonus session on the Six Human Needs** — his framework for why people sabotage themselves and what it takes to feel truly *fulfilled* even after the goals are met.
