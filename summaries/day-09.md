# Day 9 · Values &amp; Beliefs — The Hidden Drivers of Your Destiny

> 📄 **[Read the full cleaned transcript →](../transcripts/day-09.md)**  ·  🏠 **[Home](../README.md)**

**Core message:** Pain and pleasure run your behavior — but **values** are the labels you've learned to put on different levels of each, and **beliefs** are the rules that decide whether you've "hit the target." Together they steer what your brain focuses on, what it moves toward, and what it avoids. Most people never *designed* either; they inherited a jumble. Become aware of yours, and you can redesign a life you can actually win.

## The big ideas

- **Values are emotional states ranked by importance.** Given a list — success, adventure, outrageousness, love, comfort, security — everyone picks differently. We all carry a **hierarchy of values** guiding our daily focus.
- **Two kinds of values:** **moving-toward** states (love, success, adventure, security, freedom) we chase for pleasure, and **moving-away-from** states (frustration, anger, physical pain, humiliation, depression) we flee. Both are ranked in a ladder of intensity.
- **Values predict personality and destiny.** Know whether someone tops their list with *adventure* or *security* and you can roughly predict how they live, love, and respond to "let's go skydiving." Swap the top and bottom of the list and you change the whole person.
- **Conflicting values sabotage you** — the **"Gary Hart Syndrome":** two value systems pulling in opposite directions (e.g., *success* as #1 toward, *fear of rejection/failure* as #1 away). You succeed, then sabotage.
- **Means vs. ends values:** money, cars, family, relationships are *means* — you want them only for the *states* they deliver (freedom, love, security, contribution). Hunt for the **ends values** underneath.
- **Beliefs are the rules — the "if/then."** *If I make this much money, then I'm successful.* The value is the target; the belief tells you whether you've hit it. Same value, different rules = different lives.
- **Two kinds of beliefs:** **global beliefs** ("life is…, people are…, I am…") and **rules** ("if you respect me, then you'd never raise your voice"). Limiting global beliefs ("I'm not smart enough, too young, no PhD") shut down your power.
- **Where values and beliefs come from:** not a master plan — pain and pleasure delivered by parents, peers, teachers, jobs, role models, and goals you've hit. You didn't choose them; you absorbed them.
- **You can feel any state now.** Sit, breathe, and look the way you would *if* you felt successful — and you do. The states aren't locked behind achievement; bad *rules* are what make the game unwinnable.

## Memorable lines &amp; metaphors

- "You can't always control the wind, but you can control your sails."
- "**Every day above ground is a great day**" — the vivacious man whose rule for success was almost impossible to miss.
- The **Fortune 500 CEO** who felt *not* successful: rich, fit, beloved family — yet his rules ($4.5M/yr, never feel down, ~12% body fat) made winning impossible.
- "**Martians steal**" — a global belief decides which *question* you even ask: not "where did I leave my purse?" but "*which* Martian took it?"
- "Whether you believe you can or you can't — you're right." Beliefs are "the floodgate" of your power.
- **Becky &amp; Tony's respect rules:** he raises his voice to show honesty; she leaves the room to keep self-respect — "hairy fights" until they wrote *new* rules.
- "Life is not a destination… learn to enjoy the flight." / "Distinctions are power."

## The Day 9 exercise

Take out your Success Journal and:

1. **List your moving-toward values:** ask *"What's most important to me in my life?"* (or in a relationship, career, etc.) and write the states one by one. Capture **ends values** (love, passion, security), not means (money, cars, family).
2. **Rank them** — #1, #2, #3… so you can see how your brain actually prioritizes decisions.
3. **List your moving-away-from values:** the emotions you'd do almost anything to avoid (frustration, anger, humiliation, depression). **Rank these too.**
4. **Surface your rules:** under each value ask *"What has to happen for me to feel \_\_\_?"* (successful, loved). Notice where your rules make winning nearly impossible.
5. **Identify your key global beliefs** ("life is…, people are…, I am…") — especially limiting ones.
6. **Then mess with them:** rewrite rules that no longer make sense so it's *easier* to feel success/love/joy daily and *harder* to feel pain (e.g., "I'm only embarrassed if I don't live by what I believe is right").

Do as much as you want, but at minimum complete the list-and-rank step.

## Make it stick — how to apply it, plus honest context

**What modern psychology backs up**

- A ranked **hierarchy of values** echoes work on **personal values and motivation** (e.g., Schwartz's theory of basic values) — people genuinely differ in what they prioritize.
- The toward/away split maps onto **approach and avoidance motivation**, a well-studied distinction in psychology.
- "Beliefs are if/then rules that gate your feelings" is essentially **cognitive appraisal** and the core of **CBT**: it's the *interpretation*, not the event, that drives emotion. Rewriting unwinnable rules is **cognitive restructuring**.
- The "Martians steal" point is a clean illustration of **confirmation bias** — beliefs steer which questions and evidence you even consider.
- "Put your body in the state now" reflects real findings on **embodied emotion** (posture, breathing, and facial expression do influence felt emotion), though effects are modest.
- Conflicting values causing self-sabotage is a recognizable **approach–avoidance / goal conflict**.

**Where to keep your skepticism**

- The CEO and "every day above ground" stories are **vivid but unverifiable anecdotes** — take them as illustrations, not data.
- "Change your values and you change your destiny" is **directionally useful but overstated.** Awareness and reframing help, but circumstances, resources, and constraints also shape outcomes — don't read every setback as a values failure.
- "You can feel successful *right now*, just by sitting differently" is a real but **temporary** state shift, not a substitute for the slow work of changing entrenched beliefs (which Tony himself calls a multi-day process).
- The **Gary Hart** framing is Tony's pop-psychology read of a public figure, not a clinical claim.
- The **date with Destiny** seminar plug is, by his own admission, "a little commercial."

**Apply it now:** write your top five toward-values and rank them — then ask, *"What's missing?"* If *success* is #1 but *health* isn't on the list at all, you've just found a conflict worth fixing today.

**Looking ahead:** Day 9 only helps you *identify* limiting beliefs. Tomorrow Tony promises the **technology to actually change them** — how to dismantle any limiting belief and install empowering ones.
