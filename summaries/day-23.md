# Day 23 · Water-Rich Foods — Eat Live Foods to Live

> 📄 **[Read the full cleaned transcript →](../transcripts/day-23.md)**  ·  🏠 **[Home](../README.md)**

**Core message:** The quality of your life depends on the quality of the life of your *cells* — and cells thrive only when their fluid environment stays clean and oxygen-rich. The second living-health principle is to make **at least 70% of your diet water-rich foods** (fruits, vegetables, sprouts). A diet low in water content clogs the system, starves cells of oxygen, and lets toxic waste accumulate. Eat live foods if you want to be alive.

## The big ideas

- **Clean fluids, healthy cells.** Cells are constantly dying and being reborn (Tony's image: snap your fingers and ~7 billion liver cells die — but 7 billion healthy new ones are created). Their waste must be flushed out, or it accumulates in the circulatory system, reduces oxygen, and "literally poisons" you.
- **Water-rich (water-content) foods are the cleanser.** There are only three kinds on the planet — **fruits, vegetables, and sprouts** — and their juices, ideally freshly squeezed. They supply the life-giving water your body needs to carry off waste.
- **Don't try to flush by drowning.** Drinking 8–12 glasses of water a day can backfire: tap water often carries chlorine, fluoride, minerals, and other toxins. Distilled water is "often a good idea," but the amount you drink should be **dictated by thirst** — not forced. Eat your water instead.
- **The cell-life evidence.** He cites Dr. Alexander Bryce, MD (*The Laws of Life and Health*): too little fluid leaves waste only "imperfectly" cast off, so the body is poisoned by its own excretions. He frames heart disease — and the person who drops dead on a tennis court at 40 — as a lifetime of clogging the system.
- **Herbivores point the way.** Nature's biggest, most powerful animals — gorilla, elephant, rhinoceros — eat only water-rich foods, and herbivores outlive carnivores. "A building can only be as strong and as elegant as its parts."
- **It connects to the breathing principle.** This builds on the prior living-health lesson: deep breathing oxygenates the blood (at Life Mastery in Hawaii, nine days of breathing reportedly un-clumps red blood cells on the before/after blood test). Food and breath together change "the quality of life of your cells."

## The Day 23 exercise

A two-part journaling audit, then a 10-day challenge:

1. Grab your **Success Journal**. Take 2½–3 minutes and write down **every single thing that has passed your lips in the last 24 hours** — all foods, all drinks, and the amounts.
2. Now **circle everything that is water-rich / water-content** — only fruits, vegetables, sprouts, and their juices (water counts; milk does **not**, because it's thick and doesn't flow; beer does **not**, because it carries other things; nothing cooked-out counts).
3. **Estimate the percentage** of your diet that's water-rich — 80%, 70%, 50%, 25%, or 15-or-less. (In his seminars most people land around 15–20%.)
4. Then take his **10-day challenge** — combine both living-health principles:
   - **Breathe** in the ratio inhale 1, hold 4, exhale 2 — **ten times, three times a day**.
   - Make your diet **70% (or more) water content** — have a **salad with every meal**, and make **fruit your snack** instead of a candy bar.

## Make it stick — how to apply it, plus honest context

**What modern psychology backs up**

- "Eat more fruits and vegetables" is one of the most robustly supported recommendations in nutrition science, and it overlaps with how the **National Academy of Sciences** dietary guidance points away from processed, low-water foods.
- **Hydrating from food is real** — produce is genuinely high in water, and drinking "to thirst" matches current guidance better than rigid glass-counting.
- **Behavioral design works:** anchoring a single keystone habit ("salad with every meal," "fruit as the default snack") is exactly how habit research says to make change automatic — by changing the default, not relying on willpower.
- The food-audit step is a legitimate **self-monitoring** technique; simply tracking intake reliably raises awareness and nudges better choices.

**Where to keep your skepticism**

- **"15% water content is suicide"** is motivational hyperbole, not a medical finding — health is shaped by genetics, exercise, smoking, stress, and access to care, not diet alone.
- The **Alexis Carrel "immortal chicken cells kept alive 34 years"** story (he calls him a 1912 Nobel winner) is famous but **scientifically discredited** — later researchers couldn't replicate it and suspect fresh cells were inadvertently added. Treat it as a vivid parable, not proof that clean fluids make cells immortal.
- The **Bryce quotes are early-1900s** sources, and the "poisoned by its own excretions" framing is dated. The strong **"don't drink lots of water" claim is overstated** — for most people moderate water intake is fine, so don't skimp on hydration on the strength of this tape.
- His health advice tees up promotions (Mastery / Life Mastery, Power Talks, the Deepak Chopra interview). The core principle is sound; the urgency is also a **sales frame.**

**Apply it now:** do the 24-hour food audit honestly, circle the water-rich items, and commit to just one easy swap today — a salad at your next meal, fruit instead of the candy bar — plus the breathing pattern. You'll feel the energy shift faster than you expect.

**Looking ahead:** he closes by promising the *Personal Power 2* interview tape — notably **Dr. Deepak Chopra** on the **mental side of health** — reinforcing that your thoughts create biochemical change, not just what you eat and breathe.
